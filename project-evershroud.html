Genre: Pixel art action RPG

Setting: Medieval forest village

Goal: Defeat monsters and save your home

Features: Exploration, side quests, boss fights

Description

Evershroud is a 2D pixel art adventure game and my first full solo project in the genre. Inspired by classic Legend of Zelda titles, it features five handcrafted areas to explore, each filled with NPCs, quests, and secrets. Players interact with characters to uncover objectives, ultimately working toward a climactic boss battle. Along the way, you can break pots for coins, which act as a simple score and reward system.

Development Tools

Unity, VS Code, Aseprite, Photoshop

Challenges and Insights

One of the key challenges was implementing the enemy behaviour, specifically having enemies “wake up” and respond when the player entered their proximity. This logic initially worked but broke after upgrading the project to a newer version of Unity, which affected several core systems. Movement was also impacted but was successfully reworked and now functions smoothly. While Evershroud is in a solid playable state, there’s plenty of room to grow. I plan to add more enemy types, new attack mechanics, an inventory system, quest tracking, a minimap, and additional explorable areas. As my first adventure game, I'm proud of how far it's come, and excited to keep developing it into a deeper, more complete experience.
